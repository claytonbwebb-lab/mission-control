Our Story

A hidden gem in
the Yorkshire valley

2+ Acres of
grassland

Hidden in the beautiful valley of Ellerburn, Low Farm Campsite sits on over two acres of grassland on our working farm. It's a place where time genuinely stands still.

With no electric hookups and limited mobile signal, we offer something rare — a real escape. Swap the screen glow for star-filled dark skies. Swap traffic noise for a babbling beck running alongside the site.

We run from April to October with 30 pitches for tents, caravans and motor homes. Children and dogs are very welcome. Horse owners can even bring their horses — we have stables and grazing available.

On summer Saturday evenings, join us for Pizza & Puds — stone-baked pizza and fresh homemade cheesecakes, served on site. It really is that kind of place.

What's On Site

Everything you need,
nothing you don't

Off-grid doesn't mean uncomfortable. We keep it simple and natural — the way camping should be.

30 Spacious Pitches

Over two acres of grass — plenty of space between neighbours. No feeling hemmed in.

Stream on Site

A beck runs alongside the campsite — brilliant for kids with wellies and paddling adventures.

Dalby Forest Access

A direct footpath from the site straight into Dalby Forest — bike trails and walking routes on your doorstep.

Stables & Grazing

Bring your horse. We offer stables and grazing so you can explore the off-road bridle paths and local beaches.

Dark Skies

No light pollution. On clear nights the Milky Way is clearly visible — magical for stargazers of all ages.

Pizza & Puds Evenings

Summer Saturday evenings — stone-baked pizza and homemade cheesecakes, right on site. You'll smell it before you see it.

Good to Know

Our simple rules
for a great stay

Quiet from 10pm

Low noise rule from 10pm so all guests and wildlife can enjoy a peaceful stay.

No Groups or Parties

This is a peaceful site for those who want to enjoy the countryside — not a party venue.

Dogs on Leads

Working farm with children, wildlife and livestock — dogs must be on short leads at all times.

Season: April – October

We open on the second Friday in April and close at the end of October each year.
